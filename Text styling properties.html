Here, this style is called "line through". The line-through text style, also known as strikethrough, is used to indicate that text is no longer relevant, has been removed, or is incorrect. It places a horizontal line through the middle of the text, making it appear crossed out.

Again, we can see the "underline" style. The underline text style is a typographical feature that adds a horizontal line beneath the text. It is commonly used for emphasis, hyperlinks, headings, or indicating errors in handwritten documents. While underlining was widely used in typewritten documents due to the lack of bold or italic options, it is now less common in digital typography, where bold and italics are preferred for emphasis. However, it remains a key element in web design, especially for hyperlinks, and in academic or legal documents where specific formatting rules require its use.
